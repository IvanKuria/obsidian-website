The observation that the number of transistors on an integrated circuit doubles approximately every 18-24 months.

## Context

Moore's Law, proposed by Gordon Moore in 1965, has guided the semiconductor industry for decades. While transistor counts have continued to grow, the performance gains per transistor have diminished as [[Denard Scaling]] broke down. Modern chips compensate with multi-core designs and specialized accelerators.
