---
title: "Congruent Modulo"
type: definition
domain: discrete-math
aliases: [congruence modulo, modular congruence]
tags:
  - definition
---

Given integers $a$ and $b$ and $n \in \mathbb{N}$, we say that $a$ and $b$ are congruent modulo $n$ if $n | (a - b)$. We express this as $a \equiv b$ (mod n). If $a$ and $b$ are not congruent modulo $n$, we write $a \not\equiv b$ (mod n).
