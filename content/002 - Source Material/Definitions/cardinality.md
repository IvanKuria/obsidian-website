The size of a [[set]], denoted $|A|$. For a finite set, the cardinality is the number of elements it contains.

## Context

Cardinality is used throughout discrete math and computer science. For example, the set $A = \{1, 2, 3\}$ has cardinality $|A| = 3$. The concept extends to infinite sets, where sets can be countably infinite (like the integers) or uncountably infinite (like the real numbers).
