Refers to how much of a given workload is completed in a given unit of time. Often measured in bits per second (bps) for network connections.

## Context

Bandwidth is a key metric in networking and systems design. For example, a 1 Gbps Ethernet connection can theoretically transfer 1 gigabit of data per second. Higher bandwidth means more data can flow through a channel simultaneously, but it does not guarantee low [[latency]].
