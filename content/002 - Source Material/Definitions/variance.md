The variance of a random variable $X$ is:
$$Var(X) = E(X - E(X))^2$$

It measures how spread out the values of a random variable are from its expected value (mean).

## Context

Variance is one of the most important measures in statistics. For example, two datasets can have the same mean but very different variances: $\{4,5,6\}$ has low variance while $\{0,5,10\}$ has high variance. The square root of variance is the standard deviation.
