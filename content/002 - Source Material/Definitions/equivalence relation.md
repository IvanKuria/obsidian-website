A [[relation]] $R$ on a set $A$ is an equivalence relation if it is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive.

## Context

Equivalence relations partition a set into disjoint equivalence classes. For example, [[congruent modulo|congruence modulo]] $n$ is an equivalence relation on the integers: it is reflexive ($a \equiv a$), symmetric ($a \equiv b \Rightarrow b \equiv a$), and transitive.
