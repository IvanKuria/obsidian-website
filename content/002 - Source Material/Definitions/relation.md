A relation on a set $A$ is a subset $R \subseteq A \times A$. The statement $(x, y) \in R$ is often abbreviated as $xRy$, and $(x, y) \notin R$ is abbreviated as $x \not R y$.

## Context

Relations generalize the idea of "how two elements are connected." For example, "less than" on integers is a relation: $R = \{(a,b) : a < b\}$. Relations can have properties like reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity, leading to special types like [[equivalence relation|equivalence relations]].
