A set of strings (finite or infinite) over some alphabet. In the context of theory of computation, typically restricted to finite strings. [[CSE 103]]

## Context

In formal language theory, languages are the central object of study. For example, the set of all binary strings with equal numbers of 0s and 1s is a language. Languages are classified by the type of automaton or grammar needed to recognize or generate them (regular, context-free, etc.).
