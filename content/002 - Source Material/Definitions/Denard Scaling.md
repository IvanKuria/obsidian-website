---
title: "Dennard Scaling"
type: definition
domain: computer-architecture
aliases: [Denard Scaling, MOSFET scaling]
tags:
  - definition
---

A scaling principle stating that as transistors get smaller, their power density stays roughly constant because voltage and current scale downward with length. This allowed clock frequencies to increase for decades without proportional increases in power consumption. Dennard Scaling broke down around 2006 due to leakage current limits.
